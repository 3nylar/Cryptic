The index of coincidence is the probability that two letters drawn at random from a text are the same letter. In English it is about six point seven percent, and in random gibberish it is about three point eight percent. This single number tells an attacker whether a message was encrypted with one alphabet or with many, which is the first question worth asking about any classical cipher. Once the key length is known the rest of the attack is mechanical, and a computer will finish it in less time than it takes to read this sentence aloud.
